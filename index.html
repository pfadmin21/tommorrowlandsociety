Our journey

Built before the pandemic. Reimagined for Africa.

Tomorrowland is not beginning with an idea alone. It is building from operating experience, physical assets, commercial relationships and lessons earned through execution.

2017

Capital committed in one founding round

Tomorrowland began with approximately USD 225,000 in committed capital: USD 150,000 in bank financing and approximately USD 75,000 from private investors. The full capital mobilisation took place in 2017—not over several years.

2017–2020

Three active years of commercial execution

That initial capital was converted into operating capacity, physical assets, supplier relationships and paying assignments. During its first three active operating years, Tomorrowland generated approximately USD 1 million in commercial turnover.

2020

COVID interrupted the operating model

The pandemic disrupted an events-led business at the point of proven commercial traction. Tomorrowland preserved its land, infrastructure, equipment and institutional relationships rather than treating the interruption as the end of the organisation.

2026 onward

Reimagined for Africa

The cooperative is now being recapitalised around regenerative agriculture, cooperative development, project preparation, knowledge exchange and Pan-African partnerships—building from a real operating history rather than starting from zero.

The case

Africa does not lack farmers. It lacks the systems around them.

Across the continent, cooperatives are expected to organise production, maintain records, access finance, negotiate with buyers and manage logistics — often without the specialist tools or capital required to do so.

The result is a structural gap between public agricultural policy and commercial execution. Farmers remain difficult to finance, crops lose value after harvest, buyers face inconsistent supply and viable cooperatives struggle to become institutional counterparties.

The challenge is not production potential. It is investment architecture.
01 / Fragmentation

Smallholders operate alone

Weak aggregation limits purchasing power, bargaining strength and access to larger contracts.

02 / Post-harvest loss

Value disappears after harvest

Insufficient cold chain, storage, processing and logistics reduce food availability and farmer income.

03 / Finance

Capital sees risk, not evidence

Weak records, limited traceability and irregular cash flows make viable farmers difficult to underwrite.

04 / Market access

Demand remains disconnected

Without structured off-take, cooperatives struggle to serve domestic, regional and export buyers consistently.

The solution

The missing private execution layer.

Government continues to provide policy, registration, regulation and agricultural support. Cooperatives retain ownership, local governance and production.

Tomorrowland sits between them — introducing governance, digital administration, blended finance, commercial coordination, infrastructure partnerships, traceability and market access.

In practice, this follows a wholesale model already proven in the region: bulk credit and technical assistance flow to cooperatives and aggregators acting as anchor institutions, who carry the local governance, data and repayment relationship with their own members.

Eligibility

What enters the Tomorrowland pipeline.

We focus on opportunities with a real operating sponsor, a defined agricultural or food value chain and a credible path to revenue, repayment or measurable development impact.

Projects we can assess

  • A registered cooperative, enterprise, public programme or credible project sponsor.
  • A defined agricultural, food, processing, logistics or enabling-infrastructure activity.
  • An identifiable producer base, customer group or beneficiary network.
  • A clear capital requirement and proposed use of funds.
  • Basic governance, land, licensing and operating documentation.
  • A pathway to revenue, repayment, off-take or measurable impact.

What is not yet pipeline-ready

  • Ideas without an identifiable sponsor or delivery team.
  • Personal funding requests without an operating structure.
  • Projects unable to demonstrate land access or required permissions.
  • Applications with no defined budget, capital need or implementation plan.
  • Requests for guaranteed grants or automatic investment approval.
  • Transactions that cannot satisfy legal, integrity or environmental review.

Governance & confidentiality

Trust is part of the transaction.

Tomorrowland does not distribute grants or promise funding. We originate, prepare and structure credible agricultural opportunities so suitable capital providers can independently evaluate and co-invest in them.
01

Independent investment decisions

Every grant-maker, lender and investor conducts its own diligence and retains sole responsibility for its final decision.

02

Conflicts disclosed

Any ownership interest, fee arrangement, related party or dual role is identified before an opportunity is presented.

03

Controlled information sharing

Commercially sensitive materials are shared only with appropriate parties and subject to agreed confidentiality protocols.

04

Evidence before presentation

Projects advance only when minimum governance, legal, financial, operational and environmental information is available.

05

No guaranteed outcome

Acceptance into the pipeline, an indicative capital stack or introduction to a partner does not constitute a financing commitment.

06

Built toward institutional standards

Documentation, administration and compliance are structured to meet the operational diligence institutional and DFI capital providers require before committing — not only the investment case.
